The Complete Tenant Move-Out Checklist 2026

Everything you need to do before handing over your keys — room by room, step by step. Check off each item to maximize your security deposit refund and leave with zero disputes.

Updated: July 2026 Read time: 10 minutes Includes: Interactive Checklist
⚡ Quick Answer

The #1 reason tenants lose security deposit disputes is poor documentation — not actual damage. Follow this checklist exactly, and you give yourself the best possible chance of getting your full deposit back. Start at least 30 days before move-out.

Your Move-Out Timeline

30
30 Days Before

Give Written Notice & Review Lease

Submit your written move-out notice (check your lease for required notice period — usually 30–60 days). Re-read your lease to understand all move-out obligations and cleaning requirements.

14
14 Days Before

Request Pre-Move-Out Inspection

In California and many other states, you have the right to a pre-move-out walkthrough. Request this in writing so your landlord can identify issues you can fix before moving out.

7
7 Days Before

Start Deep Cleaning & Repairs

Begin thorough cleaning room by room. Make minor repairs (fill nail holes, replace light bulbs). Do NOT attempt major repairs — these can sometimes make things worse.

1
Move-Out Day

Final Walkthrough & Photo Documentation

Do a complete photo/video walkthrough of every room. Do this BEFORE handing over keys. Request a joint walkthrough with your landlord if possible.

✓
After Move-Out

Provide Forwarding Address & Monitor Deadline

Send your forwarding address in writing. Know your state's deposit return deadline. If you don't receive your deposit or itemization by then, send a demand letter immediately.

Legal Steps — Do These First

⚖️

Legal & Administrative Steps

8 items
Submit written move-out notice to landlord
Email + certified mail. Confirm required notice period in your lease first.
Critical
Re-read your lease — all move-out clauses
Note any specific cleaning requirements, painting obligations, or pet-related terms.
Critical
Request pre-move-out inspection in writing
Required to offer in California, Virginia, and other states. Gives you a chance to fix issues first.
Important
Locate and review your original move-in inspection checklist
Compare move-in condition to current condition. This is your legal baseline.
Critical
Know your state's deposit return deadline
Ranges from 14 days (NY) to 60 days (GA). Mark it on your calendar.
Critical
Prepare your forwarding address in writing
Email your forwarding address to landlord before you leave. This is legally required in most states.
Critical
Cancel or transfer utilities
Schedule cancellation for the day after move-out — not before — to avoid liability for conditions.
Standard
Forward your mail via USPS
File a mail forwarding request at least 1 week before move-out.
Standard

Kitchen Checklist

🍳

Kitchen — Complete Cleaning Guide

12 items
Clean inside oven — remove all grease and baked-on food
Use oven cleaner. Landlords frequently charge $100–$200 for oven cleaning. Do this 2 days before move-out to air out smell.
Critical
Clean stovetop — all burners, drip pans, and grates
Remove and soak burner grates. Replace drip pans if heavily stained (cheap at hardware stores).
Critical
Clean refrigerator inside and out — remove all food
Wipe all shelves and drawers. Clean door seals. Leave fridge plugged in unless otherwise instructed.
Critical
Clean dishwasher — run empty cycle with cleaner
Clean filter at bottom. Wipe door seal.
Important
Clean inside all cabinets and drawers
Wipe shelves, remove liner paper if you installed any.
Important
Clean sink — remove stains, clean drain
Use Bar Keepers Friend for tough stains. Clean garbage disposal if applicable.
Important
Clean microwave inside and out
Steam clean: boil water with lemon inside for 5 minutes, then wipe clean.
Important
Wipe down all countertops
Remove any stains. Check for damage beyond normal use.
Important
Clean range hood and filter
Soak greasy filters in hot soapy water. Replace if damaged.
Standard
Sweep and mop kitchen floor
Get corners and under appliances. Check for grease spots.
Standard
Replace any burned out light bulbs
Check under cabinet lights. Landlords regularly deduct $5–$10 per bulb.
Standard
Check for any damage to cabinets, counters, or appliances
Photograph everything, including pre-existing conditions you noted at move-in.

Bathroom Checklist

🚿

Bathroom(s) — Deep Clean Guide

10 items
Scrub toilet inside and out — including base and tank
Use toilet brush and bowl cleaner. Wipe exterior with disinfectant.
Critical
Clean bathtub/shower — remove all soap scum and stains
Use a scrub brush and bathroom cleaner. Bleach pen for grout. This is one of the most scrutinized areas.
Critical
Clean shower doors or curtain
Remove hard water deposits with white vinegar. Replace shower curtain liner if moldy (cheap and worth it).
Important
Clean and polish sink and faucet
Remove hard water spots. Clean drain of hair and debris.
Important
Clean mirror — streak-free
Use glass cleaner. Check for damage (cracks, chips) and photograph.
Standard
Wipe cabinet fronts and inside vanity
Remove all personal items. Clean interior shelves.
Standard
Clean grout lines — address any mold
Use bleach pen or grout cleaner. Surface mold can usually be removed. Document pre-existing mold from move-in photos.
Important
Sweep and mop bathroom floor
Get behind toilet and under vanity.
Standard
Replace toilet paper holder, towel bars if damaged
Loose towel bars from normal use = wear and tear. Ripped from wall = damage.
Important
Check exhaust fan — clean if dusty
Remove cover and vacuum dust. A dusty fan is often noted in inspections.
Standard

Bedrooms & Living Areas Checklist

🛋️

All Rooms — Walls, Floors & Fixtures

14 items
Fill all nail holes with spackling compound
Small nail holes from hanging pictures = wear and tear. Large holes from anchors = damage you should fix.
Important
Wipe down all walls — remove scuffs and marks
Magic Eraser works well for scuffs. Do NOT paint unless your lease requires it — unauthorized painting can be charged as damage.
Important
Vacuum carpets thoroughly — all rooms
Move furniture if possible. Vacuum edges and corners. Spot-treat any stains with carpet cleaner.
Critical
Steam clean carpets if heavily soiled or pet-stained
Professional cleaning ($150–$300) is much cheaper than landlord's charges. Keep the receipt.
Important
Mop all hard floors — wood, tile, vinyl
Use appropriate cleaner for floor type. Do not use harsh chemicals on wood floors.
Critical
Clean all windows inside — including tracks
Clean window tracks with a small brush and vacuum. Streaky windows are commonly noted in move-out inspections.
Standard
Clean window blinds — dust or wipe
Close blinds fully to dust. Check for broken slats (normal breakage is wear and tear; broken in multiple places may be damage).
Standard
Clean ceiling fan blades
Use a damp cloth. Dust buildup on fan blades is one of the most commonly missed spots.
Standard
Replace all burned-out light bulbs
Check every fixture including closets. Standard, LED — match what was there at move-in if possible.
Important
Clean inside all closets — shelves and rods
Remove all items. Wipe shelves. Check for damage to closet rod brackets.
Standard
Clean baseboards and door frames
Wipe with damp cloth. Inspectors look at baseboards closely.
Important
Check all door handles and locks work properly
Tighten loose handles with a screwdriver. Report any lock issues in writing before move-out.
Important
Test smoke and CO detectors — replace batteries
Many leases require you to maintain batteries. A dead detector during inspection is a red flag.
Important
Remove ALL personal belongings
Check all closets, cabinets, storage areas, and outdoor spaces. Abandoned items can be charged as removal fees.
Critical

Documentation Checklist — Protect Your Deposit

📸

Photo & Documentation — Most Critical Section

8 items
Photograph every room from multiple angles
Use your phone. Take wide shots and close-ups. Timestamp is automatic on most phones.
Critical
Record a video walkthrough with audio commentary
"This is the living room floor — no damage beyond normal use" etc. Date and time stamp in video.
Critical
Photograph all walls — close-ups of any marks or repairs
Show your spackling repairs. Show that walls are clean.
Critical
Photograph all appliances — inside and out
Open oven door and photograph interior. Open refrigerator. Show microwave interior.
Critical
Back up all photos to cloud storage immediately
Google Photos, iCloud, or email to yourself. If your phone is lost or stolen, your evidence is gone.
Critical
Compare move-out photos to move-in photos side by side
Any condition visible in move-in photos cannot be charged to you at move-out.
Critical
Keep receipts for any professional cleaning done
Carpet cleaning, oven cleaning, etc. These receipts can counter landlord's claimed charges.
Important
Send forwarding address to landlord via email
Email creates a timestamped record that you provided your address — legally required in most states.
Critical

Move-Out Day — Final Steps

🔑

Day Of Move-Out — Before Handing Over Keys

7 items
Do final photo/video walkthrough — BEFORE handing keys
This is non-negotiable. Once you hand the keys over, you lose control of the unit.
Critical
Request joint walkthrough with landlord if possible
Having your landlord sign off on condition at handoff is the strongest protection possible.
Important
Return ALL keys, fobs, parking passes, mailbox keys
Missing keys are commonly deducted from deposits. Count what you received at move-in.
Critical
Get written confirmation of key return
Ask for a receipt or email confirming you returned all keys on this date.
Important
Confirm move-out date in writing with landlord
Send an email: "I am surrendering possession of [address] as of [date]." This protects you from being charged extra days.
Important
Check your state's deposit return deadline one more time
Mark the deadline on your calendar. Set a phone reminder 3 days before it hits.
Critical
Keep all move-out documentation for at least 1 year
Even after you get your deposit back. If a dispute arises later, you'll want your records.
Standard
✅ The Golden Rule

Your move-out condition only needs to match your move-in condition — accounting for normal wear and tear. You are not required to return the unit to "like new" condition. If there was a scratch on the floor when you moved in and it's still there when you move out, you cannot be charged for it.

If your lease doesn't require it, normal vacuuming and spot cleaning is usually sufficient. If carpets are heavily soiled or pet-stained, professional cleaning is strongly recommended and much cheaper than landlord-arranged cleaning.

Standard repainting after a normal tenancy is considered wear and tear and is the landlord's responsibility. However, if you have significant marks, stains, or damage beyond normal scuffs, cleaning or touching up those specific areas is advisable.

Do your best and document your effort. Take before and after photos of the oven cleaning process. If your landlord charges for oven cleaning, you can dispute excessive charges — especially if you can show your move-in photos showed a dirty oven or normal cooking residue. Professional oven cleaning services typically charge $80–$150; if your landlord charges significantly more without receipts, that's disputable.

You have the right to be present for any inspection. If your landlord wants to inspect without you, politely insist on being there or request a joint walkthrough at a mutually convenient time. An inspection conducted without the tenant present — with no witness and no signed checklist — is much easier for landlords to manipulate in a dispute.
