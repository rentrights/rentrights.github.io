Can My Landlord Raise Rent Mid-Lease?

Updated June 2026  ·  8 min read  ·  All 50 States
⚡ Quick Answer

No — if you have a fixed-term lease, your landlord generally cannot raise your rent until the lease ends. On a month-to-month tenancy, they can raise it with proper written notice (usually 30–60 days). Rent control cities have additional limits on how much rent can increase.

⚖️ Not Legal Advice: Rent increase laws vary by state and city. Some areas have rent control or stabilization laws that add additional protections. Always check your local rules.

Fixed-Term Lease vs. Month-to-Month

Your rights depend entirely on what type of rental agreement you have:

📋

Fixed-Term Lease (e.g., 12-month lease)

Your rent is locked in for the entire lease period. Your landlord cannot legally raise it until the lease expires — even if costs go up, the market changes, or they simply want more money. The lease is a binding contract on both sides.

📅

Month-to-Month Tenancy

Your landlord can raise rent at the end of any month — but must give you proper written notice first (usually 30–60 days depending on your state). You can accept the increase or give notice to vacate.

✅ Bottom Line

If you signed a 12-month lease at $1,200/month, your landlord cannot charge you $1,400 in month 6. That lease price is legally binding until the lease ends. Any attempt to charge more is a breach of contract.

When Landlords CAN and CANNOT Raise Rent

✅

CAN raise rent when…

Your fixed-term lease has expired · You are on a month-to-month tenancy and proper written notice is given · Your lease specifically allows for rent adjustments (e.g., tied to CPI index) · After renewing or signing a new lease agreement

🚫

CANNOT raise rent when…

You are in the middle of a fixed-term lease · Proper written notice was not given · The increase violates local rent control limits · The increase is retaliatory (e.g., after you filed a complaint) · The increase is discriminatory under fair housing laws

Rent Control — Does It Apply to You?

Rent control or rent stabilization laws limit how much a landlord can increase rent — not just when. These laws exist at the city or county level in some states.

Cities with strong rent control include: New York City · San Francisco · Los Angeles · Washington D.C. · Portland, OR · Minneapolis, MN · Newark, NJ · Jersey City, NJ

🏙️ How to Check Your City

Search "[your city] rent control ordinance" or "[your city] rent stabilization." Your city's housing department website will have the official limits. Rent control often only applies to buildings built before a certain year (e.g., before 1978 in many cities).

How to Fight an Illegal Rent Increase

1

Check Your Lease and Notice

Pull out your lease and read it carefully. If you're in a fixed-term lease, the increase is almost certainly illegal. If month-to-month, check if proper notice was given.

2

Research Your Local Laws

Check if your city has rent control. If so, find the current allowable increase percentage. Any amount above that limit is illegal regardless of notice.

3

Send a Written Response

Write to your landlord citing the specific reason the increase is invalid — your lease term, lack of proper notice, or rent control limits. Keep a copy.

4

File a Complaint

Contact your local rent board (if rent controlled) or housing authority. In rent-controlled areas, illegal increases can result in fines and rollbacks for your landlord.

5

Do NOT Pay the Increased Amount

If the increase is illegal, continue paying your original rent amount. Document every payment. An illegal increase cannot be used as grounds for eviction if you are paying the legal rent.

Frequently Asked Questions

My landlord raised my rent by 40%. Is that legal?
In states without rent control, there is no cap on how much rent can increase — only on how much notice must be given. However, in rent-controlled cities, this would almost certainly violate local limits. Check your city's rules.

My lease is up next month. Can my landlord raise rent immediately?
Yes — when your lease expires, the landlord can propose new terms including higher rent for any renewal. You can negotiate, accept, or choose not to renew.

Can a landlord raise rent because I got a pet?
If your lease allows pets with a pet fee or increased rent, yes. If your lease doesn't address pets, adding a pet surcharge mid-lease without your agreement is generally not allowed.

My landlord verbally told me rent is going up. Is that valid?
In most states, rent increases must be in writing. A verbal notice is generally not legally sufficient. Ask for written notice.

I'm on Section 8 / housing voucher. Can my landlord raise my rent?
Yes, but increases must be approved by your housing authority and must be within fair market rent limits. The process is different from market-rate rentals.
