Common Tenant Questions

The questions every renter asks — answered clearly.

Eviction

Can my landlord evict me without going to court?

In every US state, a landlord must file a lawsuit in court before legally removing a tenant. Self-help eviction is illegal.

Read full guide → Security Deposit

How long does a landlord have to return my deposit?

Deadlines vary by state — from 14 days (Massachusetts) to 60 days (Georgia). Find your state's exact rule below.

Read full guide → Landlord Entry

Can my landlord enter my apartment without permission?

Most states require 24–48 hours notice except in genuine emergencies. Repeated unannounced entry may be harassment.

Read full guide → Repairs

My landlord won't fix the heat. What are my rights?

Landlords must maintain "habitable" conditions. If they fail, you may have the right to repair-and-deduct or withhold rent.

Read full guide → Rent Increase

Can my landlord raise rent in the middle of my lease?

No — a fixed-term lease locks in your rent. Increases are only allowed when the lease ends, and often require written notice.

Read full guide → Retaliation

Can a landlord evict me for complaining about conditions?

Retaliatory eviction is illegal in most states. If you reported a code violation, you may have strong legal protection.

Receiving this notice does NOT mean you are evicted — the landlord still has to file a court case if you don't comply.
