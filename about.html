About RentRights

RentRights was created to solve a simple problem: tenant rights information in the United States is scattered, confusing, and often written in dense legal language that the average renter cannot easily understand.

Every year, millions of renters face issues with security deposits, repairs, evictions, and landlord disputes — often without knowing what the law actually says or what options are available to them.

Our Mission: To make tenant rights information accessible, clear, and free for every renter in the United States — regardless of income, education, or legal background.

What We Do

We research landlord-tenant law across all 50 states and translate it into plain, easy-to-understand guides. Our content covers the most common issues renters face: landlord entry rights, security deposit laws, eviction procedures, repair obligations, fair housing protections, and more.

Our Approach

Every guide on RentRights is written to be practical and actionable. We don't just explain what the law says — we explain what you can actually do about it, step by step.

Not a Law Firm

RentRights is an independent educational resource. We are not a law firm, and nothing on this site constitutes legal advice or creates an attorney-client relationship. For advice specific to your situation, we always recommend consulting a licensed attorney in your state.

Free, Always

All of our guides are completely free to access. No paywalls, no signups required. We believe everyone deserves to understand their basic rights as a renter.
